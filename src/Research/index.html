The Fundamentals

What is Research?

At its core, research in computer science is the systematic investigation into new technologies, algorithms, and methodologies to establish novel facts and reach new conclusions.

While engineering focuses on using existing tools to solve known problems, research focuses on creating the tools of tomorrow. It involves deep analysis, rigorous experimentation, and contributing original knowledge to the global academic community.

Core Pillars

Discovery

Identifying gaps in current literature and exploring uncharted technological territories.

Validation

Proving hypotheses through mathematical modeling or empirical software testing.

Publication

Documenting findings in peer-reviewed journals to advance the state of the art.

Deep Understanding

The rigorous process of writing a paper forces you to understand a topic at an atomic level, transforming you from a coder into a true computer scientist.
